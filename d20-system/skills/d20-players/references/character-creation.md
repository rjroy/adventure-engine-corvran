## Step 1: Choose a Class

Every adventurer is a member of a class. A class broadly describes a character's vocation, special talents, and favored tactics.

### Class Overview

| Class     | Likes          | Primary Ability       | Complexity |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------------|------------|
| Barbarian | Battle         | Strength              | Average    |
| Bard      | Performing     | Charisma              | High       |
| Cleric    | Gods           | Wisdom                | Average    |
| Druid     | Nature         | Wisdom                | High       |
| Fighter   | Weapons        | Strength or Dexterity | Low        |
| Monk      | Unarmed combat | Dexterity and Wisdom  | High       |
| Paladin   | Defense        | Strength and Charisma | Average    |
| Ranger    | Survival       | Dexterity and Wisdom  | Average    |
| Rogue     | Stealth        | Dexterity             | Low        |
| Sorcerer  | Power          | Charisma              | High       |
| Warlock   | Occult lore    | Charisma              | High       |
| Wizard    | Spellbooks     | Intelligence          | Average    |

### Recording Class Information

When you choose a class, record:

1. **Level** - Typically start at level 1 (0 XP)
2. **Armor Training** - What armor categories you can use effectively
3. **Proficiency Bonus** - +2 at level 1

## Step 2: Determine Origin

Character origin includes background, species, and languages.

### Choose a Background

Your background represents formative experiences before adventuring. Each background provides:

- **Feat** - A special capability (see Feats)
- **Skill Proficiencies** - Proficiency in two skills
- **Tool Proficiency** - Proficiency with one tool

#### SRD Backgrounds

| Background | Skills                        | Tool            |
|------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Acolyte    | Insight, Religion             | Calligrapher's  |
| Soldier    | Athletics, Intimidation       | Gaming Set      |

### Choose a Species

Your species determines physical characteristics and special traits.

#### Species Traits Include

- **Size** - Small, Medium, etc.
- **Speed** - Base walking speed in feet
- **Special Traits** - Darkvision, resistances, special abilities

### Choose Languages

Every character knows Common plus two additional standard languages.

#### Standard Languages

| Language | Associated With  |
|----------|------------------|
| Common   | Humans, trade    |
| Dwarvish | Dwarves          |
| Elvish   | Elves            |
| Giant    | Giants, ogres    |
| Gnomish  | Gnomes           |
| Goblin   | Goblinoids       |
| Halfling | Halflings        |
| Orc      | Orcs             |
| Draconic | Dragons, kobolds |

## Step 3: Determine Ability Scores

Six abilities measure physical and mental characteristics.

### The Six Abilities

| Ability      | Abbr. | Measures                            |
|--------------|-------|-------------------------------------|
| Strength     | STR   | Physical might                      |
| Dexterity    | DEX   | Agility, reflexes, and balance      |
| Constitution | CON   | Health and stamina                  |
| Intelligence | INT   | Reasoning and memory                |
| Wisdom       | WIS   | Perceptiveness and mental fortitude |
| Charisma     | CHA   | Confidence, poise, and charm        |

### Score Generation Methods

#### Standard Array
Assign these six numbers to your abilities: **15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8**

Suggested assignments by class:

| Class     | STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Barbarian | 15  | 13  | 14  | 10  | 12  | 8   |
| Bard      | 8   | 14  | 12  | 13  | 10  | 15  |
| Cleric    | 14  | 8   | 13  | 10  | 15  | 12  |
| Druid     | 8   | 12  | 14  | 13  | 15  | 10  |
| Fighter   | 15  | 14  | 13  | 8   | 10  | 12  |
| Monk      | 12  | 15  | 13  | 10  | 14  | 8   |
| Paladin   | 15  | 10  | 13  | 8   | 12  | 14  |
| Ranger    | 12  | 15  | 13  | 8   | 14  | 10  |
| Rogue     | 12  | 15  | 13  | 14  | 10  | 8   |
| Sorcerer  | 10  | 13  | 14  | 8   | 12  | 15  |
| Warlock   | 8   | 14  | 13  | 12  | 10  | 15  |
| Wizard    | 8   | 12  | 13  | 15  | 14  | 10  |

#### Random Generation
Roll 4d6, drop the lowest die, sum the remaining three. Repeat six times.

#### Point Buy
You have 27 points. Each score starts at 8 (cost 0).

| Score | Point Cost |
|-------|------------|
| 8     | 0          |
| 9     | 1          |
| 10    | 2          |
| 11    | 3          |
| 12    | 4          |
| 13    | 5          |
| 14    | 7          |
| 15    | 9          |

Maximum score with point buy is 15.

### Background Adjustments

After assigning scores, apply background bonuses:
- Increase one of three specified abilities by 2 and another by 1, OR
- Increase all three specified abilities by 1

No score can exceed 20 from these adjustments.

### Calculate Modifiers

| Score   | Modifier |
|---------|----------|
| 1       | -5       |
| 2-3     | -4       |
| 4-5     | -3       |
| 6-7     | -2       |
| 8-9     | -1       |
| 10-11   | +0       |
| 12-13   | +1       |
| 14-15   | +2       |
| 16-17   | +3       |
| 18-19   | +4       |
| 20      | +5       |

## Step 4: Choose Alignment

Alignment describes moral and ethical outlook.

### The Nine Alignments

| Alignment      | Code | Description                                      |
|----------------|------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Lawful Good    | LG   | Fights injustice, protects the innocent          |
| Neutral Good   | NG   | Does the best they can, flexible about rules     |
| Chaotic Good   | CG   | Acts on conscience, little regard for convention |
| Lawful Neutral | LN   | Follows law, tradition, or personal codes        |
| Neutral        | N    | Avoids moral questions, does what seems best     |
| Chaotic Neutral| CN   | Values personal freedom above all else           |
| Lawful Evil    | LE   | Methodically takes what they want within rules   |
| Neutral Evil   | NE   | Pursues desires without concern for harm caused  |
| Chaotic Evil   | CE   | Acts with arbitrary violence and hatred          |

## Step 5: Fill in Details

### Saving Throws

Each class grants proficiency in two saving throws.

| Class     | Saving Throw Proficiencies |
|-----------|----------------------------|
| Barbarian | Strength, Constitution     |
| Bard      | Dexterity, Charisma        |
| Cleric    | Wisdom, Charisma           |
| Druid     | Intelligence, Wisdom       |
| Fighter   | Strength, Constitution     |
| Monk      | Strength, Dexterity        |
| Paladin   | Wisdom, Charisma           |
| Ranger    | Strength, Dexterity        |
| Rogue     | Dexterity, Intelligence    |
| Sorcerer  | Constitution, Charisma     |
| Warlock   | Wisdom, Charisma           |
| Wizard    | Intelligence, Wisdom       |

### Hit Points

#### Level 1 Hit Points

| Hit Die | Classes                                       | HP at Level 1        |
|---------|-----------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| d12     | Barbarian                                     | 12 + CON modifier    |
| d10     | Fighter, Paladin, Ranger                      | 10 + CON modifier    |
| d8      | Bard, Cleric, Druid, Monk, Rogue, Warlock     | 8 + CON modifier     |
| d6      | Sorcerer, Wizard                              | 6 + CON modifier     |

### Initiative

Initiative equals your Dexterity modifier.

### Armor Class

Without armor: **AC = 10 + DEX modifier**

With armor, see armor tables in Equipment.

### Passive Perception

**Passive Perception = 10 + Wisdom (Perception) modifier**

### Attacks

**Melee Attack Bonus** = Strength modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)

**Ranged Attack Bonus** = Dexterity modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)

### Spellcasting (If Applicable)

**Spell Save DC** = 8 + spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus

**Spell Attack Bonus** = spellcasting ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus

### Gaining a Level

1. **Adjust Hit Points** - Roll Hit Die + CON modifier (minimum 1), or use fixed value
2. **Gain Hit Die** - Add one Hit Die to your pool
3. **Record New Features** - Check class table for new features
4. **Update Proficiency Bonus** - If it increased at this level

## Multiclassing

### Prerequisites

To multiclass, you must have 13+ in the primary ability of both your current class and the new class.

| Class     | Required Ability Score |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Barbarian | Strength 13            |
| Bard      | Charisma 13            |
| Cleric    | Wisdom 13              |
| Druid     | Wisdom 13              |
| Fighter   | Strength or Dex 13     |
| Monk      | Dexterity and Wisdom 13|
| Paladin   | Strength and Cha 13    |
| Ranger    | Dexterity and Wisdom 13|
| Rogue     | Dexterity 13           |
| Sorcerer  | Charisma 13            |
| Warlock   | Charisma 13            |
| Wizard    | Intelligence 13        |

### Proficiencies Gained

When multiclassing, you gain only some proficiencies from the new class (typically limited armor, weapon, and tool proficiencies rather than full starting proficiencies).

### Hit Points and Hit Dice

- Gain Hit Points as described for levels after 1
- Track different Hit Die types separately

### Proficiency Bonus

Based on total character level, not individual class levels.
